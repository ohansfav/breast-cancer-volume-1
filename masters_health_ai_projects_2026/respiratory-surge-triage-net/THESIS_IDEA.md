# Thesis Idea: Respiratory Surge Triage Net

## Working Title
Threshold-Calibrated Respiratory Surge Triage for Outbreak Preparedness Using Interpretable Machine Learning

## Problem
During respiratory outbreaks, emergency services need fast triage systems that keep severe cases from being delayed while avoiding unnecessary overload.

## Hypothesis
A calibrated, threshold-aware baseline can prioritize severe respiratory cases in a way that is operationally useful and thesis-worthy.

## Methods
- Simulate respiratory severity data with exposure and oxygenation variables
- Train a baseline classifier
- Evaluate threshold behavior, calibration, and discrimination
- Discuss how the model could support outbreak triage policy

## Contribution
This thesis can link clinical severity scoring to public-health surge planning with a simple and explainable ML model.
