# Thesis Idea: Climate-Health Heat Risk Alert

## Working Title
Threshold-Calibrated Heat Risk Alerts for Climate Vulnerability Screening in Resource-Constrained Settings

## Problem
Heat waves are becoming more frequent and more dangerous. Public-health systems need simple models that can flag people at high risk of heat illness before emergency care demand spikes.

## Hypothesis
A calibrated, threshold-aware classifier can provide a practical heat-risk alert system with better clinical utility than accuracy-only baselines.

## Methods
- Create synthetic climate-health exposure data
- Train an interpretable machine learning classifier
- Evaluate calibration, precision-recall, and threshold behavior
- Analyze subgroup patterns by age and chronic disease burden

## Potential Contribution
This thesis can propose a low-cost risk-alert policy for climate emergencies and demonstrate how threshold tuning changes referral volume versus missed-risk tradeoffs.

## Research Outputs
- Risk score tables
- Calibration plots
- Confusion matrix analysis
- Feature importance discussion
